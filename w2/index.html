    Week 2 Report

    What I learned and did in ACIT 2811 UX/UI last week

    7 usability factors

    1. Useful - is a product/service useful
    2. Usable - is a product/service usable
    3. Findable - is a product/service findable
    4. Credible - is a product/service credible
    5. Desirable - is a product/service desirable
    6. Accessible - is a product/service accessible
    7. Valuable - is a product/service valuable

    User-Centered Design

    • Context: which the user is using the product or service
    • User Testing: feedback to validate that our assumptions are correct
    • Surveys
    • Interviews

    Iterative set of steps to solve problems that are specific to products or services for users

    1. Understand the context of use (how, where, why are people using the product/service)
    2. Specify requirements (what does the product need to do)
    3. Design solutions (to the problems that users are facing)
    4. Evaluate solutions (test, get feedback)

    Five-stage methodology

    1. Empathize - identify problems by people/users
    2. Define - analyzing/defining/articulating the problems from step 1
    3. Ideate - brainstorming, generating as many solutions as possible (non-judgemental)
    4. Prototype - pick the best solutions and prototype them
    5. Test - use the prototype to validate assumptions and get feedback

    Activity in class

    First activity: Empathize

    Imagine that you are a prospective student at BCIT and identify a challenge they might face (different from your own)

    • Define - one or two specific problems they would face
    • Ideate - brainstorm a few solutions
    • Prototype - define a possible prototype
    • Test - define how you can test the prototype

    Second activity

    Our group was assigned two factors. We had to:

    1. Give an example of a product/service that DOES fulfill a factor and one that DOES NOT
    2. Explain how those products/services could be improved
    3. Our example:
    4. Plastic bottle → accessible but not valuable (everywhere but low value)
    5. Phone → accessible and valuable (used by almost everyone)
